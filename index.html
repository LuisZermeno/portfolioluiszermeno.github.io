Radio Controlled Unmanned Ground Vehicle

Robotics and Transport.

Front view

The idea of this proyect was to create a vehicule that allows a person to see what is happening in a dangerous environment. This was specifically planned to go inside buildings after an earthquake to see if the structure of the building is safe to go inside.

Side view

The project had three main parts:

  • UI: This part was made with Python, a Raspberry Pi 4 and the radio anthena. It resembles the control of a physical control, but it was made just with a touchscreen.
  • Electronics: The radio signal needed to be read and executed the orders in a short time of response. Sending to the hardware the right movements.
  • Mechanics: The vehicle had the requirements to have torque and at the same time to be as small and light as possible.

Autonomous Mobile Robot Prototype

Robotics and computer vision

The main goals of this vehicle was to be able to follow a person by QR code in the back. The camera was an Intel-Realsense distance camera, which allowed us to know how far the person was at the same time that the reading of the position of the QR code.

After testing the functionality of the code, we step up to hardware, which we decided that needed to be four step motors. Each one with a omnidirectional wheel to be able to move the vehicle in any direction.

After that a trunk was added to carry personal items. This project was focused to help elderly in their home. The humanist side of technological projects is very important to improve the quality of life of the people, not just to be more efficient in the industry.

IoT Environment

IoT, Industry 4.0

This project was the first setp from a much greater net of little devices. The idea was to add some sensors to the office to know if someone was there. Because of the choice of home office, the flow of people is not stable. Some days the office is full and some other days without any apparent reason the office is almost empty.

This sensor allows to detect heat, it is a thermal camera. With a little bit of code and the right parameters we can know if the heat that the sensor is detecting is a person or not. This, as a first step, let us know if a room is occupied. Until know the idea is really simple. The actual project is to install more of these, along with more sensors (movement and noise sensors) and connect them to Amazon Web Services, where all the information will be processed and let us know a lot of information about the office.

To start we have two main goals, the first is to know in real time if the office is occupied, how noise there is, etc. This a easy, we just need to show the readings. The other short term goal is to analyze the data saved in AWS and seek for patterns to predict the office status. In the next image is the first tests of the thermal camera with AWS.

A long term goal is to add more sophisticated sensors and to star to control electrical devices as the doors, opening and closing from windows and lights. By sophisticated sensors it is ment to apply IA to a camera and for example know exactly who is at the office, and at which time did he or she arrived and left.
